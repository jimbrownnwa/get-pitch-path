# Email 3: Conversion Myth

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "What most businesses get wrong about {{frustration_topic}}" — recommended
Safe bet. Personalized to the lead's frustration (from Q7) — feels directly addressed to them. "What most businesses get wrong" creates curiosity without being clickbait.

### B: "The qualification trap"
Bold play. Short, vivid, mysterious. No segment personalization but the intrigue factor is high. Risk: may not clearly signal relevance to someone scanning inbox quickly.

### C: "one mistake everyone makes"
Personal, lowercase, zero-friction. High open rate but may underperform on click-through because it doesn't preview the value.

**Recommended A/B test:** A vs B
**Reason:** Tests whether personalized relevance (A) outperforms pure curiosity (B) for mid-sequence emails. Mid-sequence is where subject-line fatigue kicks in — this test shows which hook style maintains engagement.

Their lead capture asks questions. The answers get tagged. The follow-up emails reference those answers. By the time the lead hits the sales call, the rep already knows what they need, what they've tried, and what they'll pay.

Business A? Same cold leads every time. Same pitch every time. Same 3% close rate.
