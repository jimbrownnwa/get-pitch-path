# Email 4: Funnel Sketch

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "Here's what your quiz funnel would look like" — recommended
Safe bet. The "you" word pulls them in. Direct promise of a personalized deliverable. Zero ambiguity about what the email contains.

### B: "Sketching your funnel"
Bold play. Short. Implies informality and work-in-progress. Good for segments that respond to "pulling back the curtain" framing — works well for agency/consulting leads.

### C: "{{first_name}}, here's the rough version"
Personal. First-name in subject pushes open rate but can feel gimmicky if overused. Good for creator/coach segments who expect informal relationships.

**Recommended A/B test:** A vs C
**Reason:** Tests whether adding first-name personalization to an already-personalized subject provides measurable lift. Useful signal for whether to default to first-name across the sequence or reserve it for high-stakes emails.

**The quiz (8-10 questions)**
First question segments every visitor into 3-4 buckets based on what they actually need. Middle questions diagnose their specific situation. Last 2-3 questions qualify fit — budget, timeline, urgency — without feeling like an interrogation.

**Result paths (4)**
Path A: Not ready → educational nurture. We don't waste their time or yours.
Path B: Exploring → case-study style result, soft CTA.
Path C: Ready + high fit → direct booking link on the result page.
Path D: Ready + wrong fit → clean referral or decline path.

**Email sequences (one per path)**
Each sequence references their quiz answers by name. Not "thanks for subscribing." Closer to: "You said your biggest challenge is X. Here's how we'd handle that for a {{segment_noun}} like you."

**The outcome**
By the time a Path C lead lands on your calendar, they've been through 3-5 personalized emails that directly address what they told you on the quiz. The sales call starts halfway down the funnel instead of at the top. Close rates on those calls run 3-5x higher than cold inbound.
