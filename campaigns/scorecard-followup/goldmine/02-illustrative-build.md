# Email 2: Illustrative Build

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "What a quiz funnel looks like for a {{segment_noun}}" — recommended
Safe bet. Segment-personalized — they see their business type in the subject line. Direct value promise. Works across every segment variant.

### B: "The 10-day build, step by step"
Bold play. Emphasizes the speed and the transparency. "Step by step" promises specificity. Good for the capacity-frustrated segment (frustration:time).

### C: "let me show you something"
Personal, conversational, curiosity-driven. Zero friction. Good for creator/coach segments that respond to informal one-on-one tone.

**Recommended A/B test:** A vs C
**Reason:** Tests segment-specific framing (A) against curiosity-driven informality (C). Reveals whether the Goldmine audience clicks on relevance or on intrigue — informs whether to personalize more or hook harder in future emails.

Picture a {{segment_example}} with a similar profile to yours. Traffic in the same range. Offers in a similar mix. Static opt-in form converting at 2.1%. Leads come in cold — nobody knows what they want, sales calls start from zero every time.

**The quiz.** 8-10 questions that map to their buyer's actual decisions. Not "what's your email." More like "what's your biggest challenge right now" and "what have you already tried." Every question earns its place.

**The branching.** Each answer routes the visitor toward the right result. A ready-to-buy visitor takes a different path than a tire-kicker. They don't see each other's content.

**The email sequences.** One per result path. Each sequence references the lead's exact quiz answers: "You said your biggest challenge is X. Here's how we'd handle that for a {{segment_noun}} like you."

**The wiring.** Quiz feeds email tool. Email tool feeds calendar. One system. No leaks.

Typical result across industries: opt-in rate climbs from 2% to 18-24%. Sales calls start halfway down the funnel because the lead has already told you what they need, what they've tried, and what they'll pay.
