# Email 1: Result Delivery

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "Your scorecard: Conversion Goldmine" — recommended
Direct and matches exactly what they were promised. Highest open rate for delivery emails because the expectation set at quiz completion is being fulfilled. The word "Goldmine" carries implicit positive framing — they feel rewarded for completing the quiz.

### B: "You're leaving {{projected_revenue}}/mo on the table"
Bold play. Specific dollar amount creates immediate curiosity and stakes. Harder-hitting but riskier — if the number ends up lower than they expected (mid-LTV, lower traffic), it can feel underwhelming. Best for high-LTV segments.

### C: "hey — here's what your scorecard says"
Personal, lowercase, feels like an email from a friend rather than a report. High trust signal for creator/coach audiences. Lower open rate than A but higher reply rate.

**Recommended A/B test:** A vs B
**Reason:** Tests whether the Goldmine tier responds better to the label ("Conversion Goldmine" — identity/badge framing) or to the math (specific dollar amount — loss-aversion framing). Result informs subject-line strategy for emails 3-5, which lean on both framings.

## Send Timing
Day 0 — Immediately at quiz completion.
Delivery emails must land within seconds of the trigger. Quiz takers are in a peak-engagement state right after submitting; open rates on delivery emails regularly exceed 85%. Any delay past 60 seconds measurably reduces that.
