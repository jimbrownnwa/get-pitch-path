## Email Copy

{{first_name}},

Opt-in forms convert at 2% on average. That means 980 out of every 1,000 visitors leave without giving you anything.

Why?

Because an opt-in form asks for a transaction (your email for our guide) before the visitor has any reason to trust you. They don't know if the guide is worth the trade. They have no context on who you are. And "enter your email" is the single least interesting thing you can ask someone to do.

A quiz flips all of that.

You start by asking a question about them, not you. "What's your biggest challenge right now?" That's a question they want to answer — because the answer tells them something about themselves, and because the next page will (implicitly) speak to what they said.

Every question they answer increases their investment in the outcome. By question 5 or 6, they're committed. By the time you ask for an email to deliver the result, they've already paid in time and attention.

That's why quiz funnels convert at 15-30%. Not magic. Psychology.

Next email: a teardown of what a quiz funnel looks like for different business types.

— Jim

---

**P.S.** The "sunk cost" dynamic here is real — and it works because the quiz itself delivers value (a personalized result), so the user's investment is rewarded.
