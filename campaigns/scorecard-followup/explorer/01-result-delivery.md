# Email 1: Result Delivery

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "Your scorecard: Early Explorer"
Label-based. "Early Explorer" can feel like being told you're not ready.

### B: "The quick version on quiz funnels" — recommended
Value-forward. Sidesteps the label entirely. Promises education.

### C: "hey — thanks for taking the scorecard"
Personal, grateful, low-stakes.

**Recommended A/B test:** B vs C
**Reason:** Value-delivery vs relationship-building. For the lowest-tier result, avoid leading with the label.

## Email Copy

{{first_name}},

Your scorecard: Early Explorer.

Translation: you're researching. Maybe you just want to understand what a quiz funnel is. Maybe you're sizing this up for a client. Maybe you're not sure this applies to your business yet.

That's a smart place to be. Pretending to be further along than you are is how money gets wasted.

Here's the quick version.

A quiz funnel replaces static opt-in forms with an interactive experience. Instead of "enter your email for a free guide," visitors answer 8-12 questions about their specific situation. They get a personalized result. Each answer gets tagged, and they're routed into a follow-up sequence tailored to what they told you.

Why it matters: businesses that use quiz funnels see 5-10x higher opt-in rates than static forms. People would rather answer questions about themselves than download yet another PDF.

Over the next month I'll send a few emails that break down what quiz funnels look like across different industries. You'll see concrete examples, not marketing fluff.

First one lands in a few days. Check your inbox.
