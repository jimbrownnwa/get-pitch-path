# Email 1: Result Delivery

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "Your scorecard: Foundation Builder"
Label-based, but "Foundation Builder" is less flattering than higher tiers.

### B: "Here's your 90-day roadmap" — recommended
Bold play. Reframes the result as a plan, not a verdict. Better open rate for a result that could feel like rejection.

### C: "hey — here's your scorecard"
Personal, neutral.

**Recommended A/B test:** B vs C
**Reason:** For a lower-tier result, the subject shouldn't lean on the label. Test whether reframe (B) or informality (C) wins.

## Email Copy

{{first_name}},

Your scorecard: Foundation Builder.

Honest read: you're earlier in the journey. That's fine — most businesses are, most of the time.

A quiz funnel works best when you have traffic to drive through it and offers to route people toward. You may need to build one or both of those foundations first. That's what a Foundation Builder result means.

Here's the 90-day roadmap:

**Month 1: Clarify the core offer.** Start driving traffic — even 500 visitors/mo is enough to start. Pick one channel and go deep.

**Month 2: Build the lead capture.** This is where a quiz funnel slots in later. For now: focus on getting opt-ins at all.

**Month 3: Launch the quiz funnel.** At this point you have traffic and offers. The quiz funnel is the conversion engine that makes both work harder.

You don't need to hire anyone today. But when you hit month 3, the quiz funnel build is a 10-day project. Pitchpath handles the whole thing.

Over the next month I'll send a few emails with things to work on while you build the foundation.

Reply anytime with questions — I read every email.
