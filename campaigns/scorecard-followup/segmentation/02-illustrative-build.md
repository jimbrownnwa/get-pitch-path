## Email Copy

{{first_name}},

Two days ago: Segmentation Opportunity.

Here's what segmentation looks like in practice.

Picture a {{segment_example}} with your profile. 3 offers at different price points. Current funnel: one landing page, one email list, one welcome sequence.

Everyone gets the same treatment. The ready-to-buy prospect gets the same intro email as the just-curious visitor. The high-budget lead gets the same pitch as the bargain hunter.

Here's the rebuild:

**Quiz-based front door.** Every visitor answers 7-9 questions that reveal fit, readiness, and need.

**Routing.** Answers trigger tags. Tags route the lead to the right path.

**Three distinct paths:**
Path 1 → Low-intent explorer → Educational sequence, no pitch for 2 weeks
Path 2 → Mid-intent evaluator → Case studies, proof points, soft CTA
Path 3 → High-intent ready-to-buy → Direct offer, booking link on the result page

**Email sequences tailored to each path.** The evaluator gets proof. The ready-to-buy gets the offer.

What changes: every lead gets the right pitch at the right time, without you lifting a finger. Average conversion across the funnel typically doubles.

→ [See How Segmentation Works]

— Jim
