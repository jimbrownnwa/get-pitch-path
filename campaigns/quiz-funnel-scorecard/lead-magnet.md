## Segment Blueprints

Each result profile includes a "Your quiz funnel blueprint" section that varies by Q1 business type. Below are the segment-specific blueprints.

### Blueprint: Course Creator / Digital Product (segment:creator)

**Quiz type:** "Which [product/course] is right for you?"

Example architecture:
- 8-10 questions about their current skill level, goals, budget, and learning style
- 3-4 result paths: Beginner → Starter course, Intermediate → Main course, Advanced → Premium/coaching, Not ready → Free resource
- Each result page shows the recommended product with social proof specific to that level
- Follow-up emails reference their quiz answers: "You said your biggest challenge is [X]. Here is how [product] addresses that."

Why it works for creators: Most course creators pitch one product to everyone. A quiz funnel lets you recommend the RIGHT product and make each person feel like you built it for them.

---

### Blueprint: E-Commerce / DTC (segment:ecom)

**Quiz type:** "Find your perfect [product]" or "What does your [skin/body/home] need?"

Example architecture:
- 7-9 questions about their preferences, needs, skin type, lifestyle, budget
- 4-6 result paths: each recommends a specific product or bundle
- Result pages include product images, reviews from similar customers, and a direct add-to-cart CTA
- Follow-up emails: abandoned quiz recovery, product education, cross-sell based on quiz answers

Why it works for e-com: Product recommendation quizzes reduce choice paralysis. Instead of browsing 50 products, the customer answers 7 questions and gets told "this is the one for you." Conversion rates on personalized recommendations are 3-5x higher than browse-and-buy.

---

### Blueprint: Local Service Business (segment:local)

**Quiz type:** "What [treatment/service] is right for you?" or "Get your personalized [treatment] plan"

Example architecture for medspas:
- 8-10 questions about their concerns (aging, acne, body contouring), budget, timeline, previous treatments
- 3-5 result paths: each recommends a treatment category with pricing range
- Result page includes before/after examples for their specific concern
- Follow-up: personalized treatment guide, booking CTA, intro-offer for their recommended service

Example architecture for home services:
- 6-8 questions about project type, scope, timeline, budget
- Result paths: each gives an estimated scope and recommended service tier
- Follow-up: project planning guide, free estimate CTA

Why it works for local: Local service businesses sell expertise and trust. A quiz that diagnoses the client's need and recommends a specific treatment/service feels like a consultation — and builds trust before they ever walk in the door.

---

### Blueprint: Coaching / Consulting (segment:coach)

**Quiz type:** "What is your [business/life/health] score?" or "Discover your [type/stage]"

Example architecture:
- 10-12 questions assessing their current situation across multiple dimensions
- 4-5 result profiles: each maps to a coaching tier or program
- Result page shows their score with specific strengths and gaps
- Follow-up: detailed breakdown of their weak areas, case study of someone in their profile who got results, booking CTA for discovery call

Why it works for coaches: Coaches sell transformation. A scorecard that shows someone exactly where they stand — with specific gaps identified — creates the "I need help" moment that books discovery calls. The quiz IS the sales conversation, automated.

---

### Blueprint: Agency / B2B Services (segment:agency)

**Quiz type:** "How does your [marketing/operations/tech stack] score?" or "Get your custom [audit/assessment]"

Example architecture:
- 10-15 questions assessing their current setup, team size, budget, goals
- 3-4 result tiers: DIY recommendations for low-score, managed service pitch for high-score
- Result page includes a mini-audit with specific findings
- Follow-up: detailed findings report, case study from their industry, proposal request CTA

Why it works for agencies: B2B buyers do extensive research before buying. A scorecard that gives them genuine diagnostic value — for free — positions the agency as the expert. The quiz data also pre-qualifies the lead so the sales call starts further down the funnel.

## Implementation Notes

### Recommended Build Tool
This quiz should be built using whatever quiz platform Pitchpath uses internally — or built custom. Since Pitchpath IS a quiz funnel service, this lead magnet should be the best possible demonstration of the product. It should not look or feel like a Typeform. It should feel like a Pitchpath quiz funnel.

### Design Requirements
- Clean, minimal design — the content does the work
- Progress bar showing question count
- One question per screen (no scrolling multi-question forms)
- Mobile-first — majority of social/ad traffic will be mobile
- Result page should feel like a personalized report, not a generic summary
- Brand colors and typography consistent with getpitchpath.com

### Integration Requirements
- Email ESP integration for immediate result delivery + sequence triggers
- Segmentation tags passed to ESP (see tags above)
- UTM parameter capture for attribution (which ad, social post, or content piece drove the quiz taker)
- Optional: Pixel/analytics integration for retargeting quiz starters who didn't finish

### Conversion Optimization
- Exit intent on quiz pages: "You are X questions away from your scorecard"
- Abandoned quiz email: if they give email on Q1 landing page but don't finish
- Social proof on result pages: "X people have taken this scorecard"
- Share prompt after results: "Share your scorecard on LinkedIn/Twitter"
