## Brand

**Name:** Pitchpath
**Domain:** getpitchpath.com
**Model:** Done-for-you service (NOT SaaS, NOT a tool)
**Offer:** Full-service interactive quiz funnel system — includes the quiz/funnel build AND the backend email follow-up and sequencing. Client gets a complete conversion machine, we do all the work.

---

## Primary Positioning

Angle: THE DONE-FOR-YOU CONVERSION EXPERIENCE (recommended primary)

Statement: Every quiz tool on the market hands you a builder and wishes you luck. Pitchpath builds the entire conversion experience for you — the quiz, the funnel, the branching logic, and every follow-up email. You get customers. We do the work.

Psychology: The market is saturated with DIY quiz tools all screaming "5x more leads" and "no-code builder." But the dirty secret is that most businesses buy these tools, build a mediocre quiz, and get the same 2% opt-in rate they started with. Pitchpath doesn't compete with tools — we compete with the time, expertise, and effort it takes to build a quiz funnel that actually converts. The enemy is DIY, not other services.

Headline direction: "Quiz tools give you a builder. We give you a quiz funnel that converts — built, launched, and converting in 10 days."

Best for: Course creators, coaches, info-product sellers, and e-commerce brands who already have something to sell and traffic to drive — they just need a conversion system that works. They have tried (or considered) DIY quiz tools and either failed or don't want to spend the time.

Proof needed: Before/after conversion lifts from real client builds, full-funnel revenue attribution (not just opt-in rate), 3-5 named case studies with dollar figures, specific deliverable descriptions (number of questions, result paths, emails in the sequence).

## Competitive Landscape Summary

Sophistication: Stage 3-4 — Crowded DIY tool market, but done-for-you service is a category of one.

Primary alternative: Buying Interact or ScoreApp, watching 3 YouTube tutorials, building a mediocre quiz over a weekend, connecting it to Mailchimp with a generic welcome sequence, and getting a 2-4% opt-in rate. The "DIY + hope" pattern.

### Every competitor is a DIY tool. This is the key insight.

Competitors analyzed (live, April 2026):
- Typeform — "Build forms at the drop of a prompt" / DIY form builder / $29-99/mo
- Interact — "Find your perfect lead generation quiz" / DIY quiz builder / $39-209/mo
- ScoreApp — "Scorecard Funnels That Qualify Your Leads" / DIY scorecard builder
- Outgrow — "Boost Your Marketing With Quizzes, Calculators And Forms" / DIY builder / 1000+ templates
- Involve.me — "AI quizzes, forms and calculators" / DIY builder / AI-assisted
- LeadQuizzes — "5x More Leads With AI-Powered Quizzes" / DIY builder

**What they ALL have in common:**
- Self-serve SaaS model (you build it yourself)
- Template libraries and drag-and-drop builders
- No backend email strategy included
- No conversion optimization expertise included
- No branching-logic strategy included
- No follow-up sequencing — "integrate with your ESP" and figure it out

**What NONE of them offer:**
- A done-for-you build by people who do this every day
- The quiz AND the email sequences as one system
- Conversion strategy baked in (not just a tool to execute your own strategy)
- Full-funnel architecture: quiz → segmentation → personalized follow-up → sale
- Accountability for results (a tool can't be accountable; a service can)

Saturated claims (dead territory — do not echo):
- "No-code drag-and-drop builder"
- "AI-powered quiz / form / lead gen"
- "5x / 15x / 500% more leads"
- "Qualify your leads automatically"
- "All-in-one platform"
- "Trusted by [N,000] businesses"
- "1000+ templates"

White space (owned by Pitchpath):
- Done-for-you quiz funnel builds (nobody else does this as a productized service)
- Quiz + email sequences as a single integrated deliverable
- "We do the work" positioning against DIY fatigue
- Full-funnel conversion attribution (not just opt-in rate)
- The quiz as a micro-salesperson that segments AND sells
- Expert-built branching logic (the part DIY users get wrong)

## All Angles Explored

### Angle 1: THE DONE-FOR-YOU CONVERSION EXPERIENCE (★ selected primary)
- Statement: Every quiz tool hands you a builder and wishes you luck. Pitchpath builds the entire conversion experience for you — the quiz, the funnel, the branching logic, and every follow-up email.
- Psychology: Reframes the competitive set. We don't compete with Interact or ScoreApp — we compete with the time and expertise it takes to use them well. This sidesteps every feature-comparison conversation.
- Headline: "Quiz tools give you a builder. We give you customers."
- Best for: Primary positioning across all channels. Landing page hero. Core brand message.
- Why this is the pick: It turns every competitor into a foil. The more quiz tools flood the market, the more people realize they need someone to actually build the thing. We are that someone.

### Angle 2: THE ANTI-DIY
- Statement: You could build a quiz funnel yourself. You could also do your own taxes. We are what happens when you stop pretending you have time for this.
- Psychology: Targets the guilt and frustration of DIY attempts. Every marketer has a half-built Typeform quiz sitting in a tab somewhere. This angle names that pain.
- Headline: "Your half-built Typeform quiz is judging you. Let us build the real thing."
- Best for: Ads, social hooks, cold outreach, retargeting people who visited DIY quiz tool sites. High-humor, high-contrast angle.
- Why not primary: Slightly negative — works best as a campaign hook, not a brand-level position.

### Angle 3: THE COMPLETE SYSTEM
- Statement: Other services build you a quiz. We build you a system — the quiz, the segmentation logic, and every follow-up email tailored to what each person told you.
- Psychology: Highlights that a quiz alone is worthless without the backend. The value is in the SYSTEM (quiz + email sequences + segmentation), not just the quiz. Targets buyers who have been burned by partial solutions.
- Headline: "A quiz without follow-up emails is a survey. We build the complete system."
- Best for: Sophisticated marketers, email-savvy audiences, course creators who already have a list. Great for email campaigns and LinkedIn.
- Why not primary: Requires more explanation — "complete system" is less instantly clear than "done for you." Strong secondary angle for warmer audiences.

### Angle 4: THE 2-MINUTE SALES CONVERSATION
- Statement: The best salespeople ask questions before they pitch. Your landing page should do the same — and we build the conversation.
- Psychology: Anchors the product to a universally understood sales truth — discovery before prescription. Makes the quiz feel like a consultation, not a form.
- Headline: "Your best salesperson asks 5 questions before quoting a price. We build your landing page to do the same."
- Best for: High-ticket coaches, consultants, service businesses, B2B. Audiences that think in terms of "sales process," not "funnels."
- Why not primary: Narrower audience. Excellent for a service-business-specific campaign.

### Angle 5: THE RESULTS-OR-NOTHING OPERATOR
- Statement: We do not sell quiz funnels. We sell the conversion lift that comes after. If the numbers do not move, we have not done our job.
- Psychology: Radical accountability. In a market full of tools that shrug when your quiz converts at 2%, a service that ties itself to results stands out. This is the trust angle.
- Headline: "Quiz tools promise leads. We promise a conversion lift — because we do the work."
- Best for: Skeptical buyers, people burned by tools or agencies, high-ticket prospects who want guarantees. Sales calls, case study pages, bottom-of-funnel content.
- Why not primary: Only credible once Pitchpath has 5+ case studies with real numbers. Park this for later — it becomes the strongest angle once proof exists.

## Why ★ The Done-For-You Conversion Experience

At Stage 3-4 sophistication in the quiz tool market, every incumbent is screaming about features, templates, and "Nx more leads." Pitchpath sidesteps the entire fight by not being a tool at all.

Three strategic advantages:

1. **Every competitor becomes a foil.** The more DIY quiz tools launch, the more noise there is, and the more valuable "we just build it for you" becomes. Pitchpath gets stronger as the market gets more crowded.
2. **Higher price point, less price sensitivity.** A done-for-you service naturally commands premium pricing. We are not competing at $39/mo against Interact — we are competing against the cost of a marketing hire or agency retainer.
3. **Full-funnel attribution.** Because we build the quiz AND the email sequences, we can claim the entire conversion path — from click to customer. No quiz tool can do this because they hand off to a separate ESP and lose the thread.

The other four angles are strong campaign variants under this umbrella. The Anti-DIY is the best ad hook. The Complete System is the best angle for email audiences. The 2-Minute Sales Conversation is best for high-ticket B2B. The Results-or-Nothing Operator becomes primary once case studies accumulate.
