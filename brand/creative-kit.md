# Pitchpath — Brand Creative Kit

> Visual DNA for all Pitchpath creative assets. Every mode in /creative reads this file before generating. Every designer, developer, and skill references this for visual consistency.

---

## Brand Colors

| Role | Hex | Name | Usage |
|------|-----|------|-------|
| Primary | #0F172A | Deep Navy | Dark base. Backgrounds, hero sections, cards. |
| Secondary | #1E293B | Slate | Panels, secondary backgrounds, layering within dark theme. |
| Accent | #F97316 | Pitch Orange | CTAs, highlights, data points, key numbers, interactive elements. |
| Success | #22C55E | Proof Green | Conversion metrics, positive results, checkmarks, before/after gains. |
| Text | #F8FAFC | Near White | Primary body text on dark backgrounds. High contrast. |
| Text Muted | #94A3B8 | Cool Gray | Secondary text, labels, captions, supporting copy. |

### Color Rules

- Dark backgrounds are the default. Light backgrounds are the exception (email, PDF exports where dark is impractical).
- Pitch Orange is reserved for actions and emphasis. Never use it as a background fill.
- Proof Green appears only with real numbers and results. Do not use it decoratively.
- Maximum 3 colors in any single composition: base + text + one accent.
- No gradients between brand colors. Flat, solid fills only. Subtle opacity variations (10-20%) are acceptable for layering.

## Typography Direction

### Headlines
- **Style:** Bold, modern sans-serif. High impact.
- **References:** Inter Bold, Space Grotesk Bold, or similar geometric sans.
- **Treatment:** Tight letter-spacing (-0.02em to -0.04em). All-caps for hero statements. Sentence case for section headers.
- **Sizing:** Large and commanding. Hero headlines at 48-72px. Section headers at 24-32px.

### Body
- **Style:** Clean, readable, neutral weight.
- **References:** Inter Regular, system sans-serif stack.
- **Treatment:** 16-18px base. Line height 1.6-1.7. Generous paragraph spacing — the voice uses white space, the typography supports it.

### Data / Numbers
- **Style:** Tabular or monospace numerals.
- **Treatment:** Numbers are heroes in Pitchpath copy. They should feel precise and engineered. Use the accent color (Pitch Orange) for key metrics. Larger font size than surrounding text when displaying conversion rates, revenue figures, or percentages.
- **References:** JetBrains Mono, SF Mono, or tabular figures from Inter.

### Typography Rules
- Never use more than 2 typefaces in one composition (headline + body).
- Never use script, serif, or decorative fonts. Pitchpath is an operator brand.
- Bold is for emphasis. Italic is never used for emphasis — rely on sentence construction (per voice profile).
- Text on dark backgrounds is always Near White (#F8FAFC) or Cool Gray (#94A3B8). Never use Pitch Orange for body text.

## Visual Style

### Photography
- No stock photos. Ever. Not even "good" stock photos.
- Primary visual content: screenshots of real quiz funnels, dashboard data, conversion metrics, funnel architecture diagrams.
- If people appear: real founders at real desks, natural lighting, candid. Not models, not studios, not handshake shots.
- Screenshot style: clean browser frames, dark UI, data visible. The work product IS the visual.

### Illustrations
- Minimal. If used, flat and geometric — diagrams, flowcharts, funnel architectures.
- Functional, not decorative. Every illustration communicates a concept or process.
- Color palette: Deep Navy base with Pitch Orange highlights and Cool Gray supporting lines.
- No hand-drawn style. No whimsical illustrations. No character illustrations.

### Icons
- Simple, line-weight consistent, geometric.
- Single color (Near White or Pitch Orange on dark backgrounds).
- Functional — used for navigation and labeling, not decoration.

### Data Visualization
- Conversion metrics and before/after comparisons are the signature visual.
- Bar charts, simple line charts, and large standalone numbers.
- Always: Proof Green for positive metrics, Pitch Orange for key data points.
- Never: pie charts, 3D charts, or decorative infographics.

### Mood
"Senior consultant's slide deck at 11pm."
- Dark, clean, data-forward.
- Confident without trying to look cool.
- Premium but not luxury. Serious but not corporate.
- The design says "we do serious work" the way the voice says "here are the numbers."

### Signature Visual Patterns
1. **Before/after conversion comparison.** Static form at 2% vs. Pitchpath quiz funnel at 28%. The number IS the visual. Use Proof Green for the Pitchpath number.
2. **Funnel architecture diagram.** Clean flowchart showing quiz → branching logic → segmented email paths → conversion. Shows the complexity of what we build.
3. **Quiz screen mockup.** A single quiz question on a dark UI, clean typography, progress bar. Shows the product experience.
4. **Client result callout.** Large number (e.g., "14.2%") in Pitch Orange, context line below in Cool Gray. Used in social, ads, case studies.

## What to Avoid

- Stock photography (smiling people pointing at screens, handshakes, whiteboard brainstorming)
- Gradient overload (the 2020 SaaS aesthetic — purple-to-blue hero sections)
- Rounded, bubbly, "friendly" UI elements (pill buttons, cartoon illustrations, confetti)
- Neon or fluorescent accent colors
- Illustrations that look like every other SaaS landing page (Humaaans, Undraw, etc.)
- Purple/blue gradient hero sections
- Confetti, sparkles, celebration graphics, or party metaphors
- Any visual that could belong to Typeform, Interact, or ScoreApp
- Light/white default backgrounds (competitors own that space — we are dark-first)
- Decorative elements that do not communicate information
- Busy compositions with multiple focal points
- Low-contrast text on any background

## Competitor Visual Differentiation

### Typeform
Clean and friendly. Rounded corners, light backgrounds, playful micro-interactions. Too soft for Pitchpath. Their aesthetic says "forms can be fun." Ours says "funnels should convert."

### ScoreApp
Blue/white, corporate SaaS feel. Trust badges, feature grids, standard B2B layout. Competent but forgettable. Our dark theme and data-forward design differentiates immediately.

### Interact
Warm, creator-friendly, light backgrounds with coral/pink accents. Aimed at beginners and small creators. We target operators — our design should feel more senior and conversion-focused.

### Outgrow
Template-heavy, feature-dense, busy layouts. Tries to show everything at once. Pitchpath shows one thing at a time — confidence in restraint.

## Asset Generation Notes

When generating creative assets with /creative or any design tool:

1. **Always start from the dark base.** Design on #0F172A, not white.
2. **One focal point per composition.** A number, a screenshot, or a headline. Never all three competing.
3. **Text space matters.** Leave room for copy — the voice profile's short-sentence hammers need breathing room.
4. **Mobile first.** 70%+ of social and ad traffic is mobile. If it doesn't work at 375px wide, it doesn't work.
5. **The number is the hero.** When showing results, the conversion number is the largest element. Context is secondary.

## AI Image Generation Notes (for /creative with Replicate)

When generating images via AI models:

**Prompt keywords to include:**
- "dark background, deep navy (#0F172A)"
- "minimal, clean, professional"
- "warm orange accent highlights"
- "data visualization, conversion metrics"
- "editorial photography style" (for any people shots)
- "modern sans-serif typography"

**Prompt keywords to exclude (negative prompt):**
- "stock photo, generic, corporate"
- "bright, colorful, playful, whimsical"
- "gradient, neon, fluorescent"
- "cartoon, illustration, hand-drawn"
- "busy, cluttered, multiple focal points"
- "light background, white background"
